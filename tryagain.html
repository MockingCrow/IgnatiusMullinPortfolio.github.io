Try Again

Level Design and Scripting

Try Again is a 2.5D “spectacle platformer” about a video game crash test dummy named Benny, who is trapped inside of an unfinished game by a mysterious, Oz-like figure called, “The Designer.” Benny needs you to get him out of this Sisyphean grey-box level design facility.

My Role

  • Design the platforming for Act 2 of the game
  • Arrange key scripted narrative and gameplay moments to fit in well with overall flow of the level
  • Create prototypes of new gameplay mechanics and level ideas
  • Code auxiliary systems to support design mechanics

Contribution

  1. Subway Level Platforming

    Using the Unity engine, I laid out the framework for the platforming of the subway section. This includes the basic platforming of the level (vaults, jumps, slides, etc.), as well as scripting larger events such as the movement of subway cars which act as hazards and platforms for the player. Additionally, I participated in merging my platforming framework with the train and train station models created by our art team. I am especially proud of the wall running section I created in the second half of the section which was the first extended wall running section that was added into the game.

  2. Hit Indicator System

    I created a hit indicator tool coded in C# that allowed team members to easily add hit indicators into the game. Users could insert a hit indicator image of their choice. Unity gizmos were used to allow positioning of the hit indicators in the scene from within the editor window.
